Alcohol can hurt and scar your liver.

Drinking can scar your liver. This can begin during the teen years.

  • More than 2 million Americans have alcohol-related liver disease.
  • Some drinkers develop alcoholic hepatitis, or inflammation of the liver.
  • This can result in fever, jaundice (abnormal yellowing of the skin, eyeballs, and urine), abdominal pain, death.
  • About 10 to 20 percent of heavy drinkers develop alcoholic cirrhosis, or scarring of the liver.
  • This can cause death, even if drinking stops.
