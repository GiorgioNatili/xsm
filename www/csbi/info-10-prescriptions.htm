Drug overdoses now cause more unintentional deaths than motor vehicle crashes.


An estimated 71,000 children are seen in emergency rooms each year because of medication overdose (excluding suicide attempts).

    When abused:

  • Pain killers like OxyContinTM (OC’s) or Vicodin can cause addiction, brain damage, sudden death, and can lead to heroin use.
  • Stimulant medications like Ritalin, Adderall, and Dexedrine can cause addiction, seizures, rapid heart beat, and sudden death.
  • Tranquilizers like Klonopin or Xanax can cause addiction, drowsiness, accidents, and death from respiratory depression.
  • Dextromethorphan (DXM), an ingredient commonly found in cough medicine can cause rapid heart beat, nausea, urinary retention, and psychosis.
