During your teenage years, alcohol and drugs can lead to long-term damage in your liver.


More than 2 million Americans have alcohol-related liver disease, like cirrhosis (scarring of the liver). This can result in fever, jaundice (abnormal yellowing of the skin, eyeballs, and urine), abdominal pain, and even death.


Drug and alcohol use increases your chance of getting hepatitis and bleeding in the digestive system.
