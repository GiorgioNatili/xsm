Tap on the five colored regions to see how alcohol and drugs affect your brain:


Prefrontal Cortex: Interferes with making wise decisions.
Motor Cortex: Can cause a stroke leading to paralysis.
Reward Pathway: Gets hijacked, so you only feel good when you are on drugs, and you keep craving more.
Hippocampus: Hurts your ability to learn and remember.
Cerebellum: Impairs balance and coordination, affects emotions.

Your brain is going through important development all the way into your mid-20’s!

To help reach your goals, and be the best YOU that you can be, you need a healthy brain.

Because your brain is still developing, it is extremely sensitive to substance use, and can get more easily addicted than adult brains.

Here are some important parts of your brain, and explanations of what they do:

  • Prefrontal Cortex: Important for decision making, planning ahead and self-control
  • Hippocampus: Responsible for memory
  • Reward Pathway: Involved in feelings of reward, pleasure, and motivation
  • Cerebellum: Affects balance and coordination
  • Motor Cortex: Controls movement
